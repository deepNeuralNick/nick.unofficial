### How to Calculate Your Averageness Score:
For each major facial feature, measure how far you deviate from the population average:

$$\text{Feature Deviation} = \frac{|\text{Your Measurement} - \text{Population Average}|}{\text{Population Standard Deviation}}$$

$$V = 1 - \text{Average}(\text{All Feature Deviations})$$

**Translation:** If your facial features are close to the mathematical average for your population, you score high. If you're "unique" and "distinctive," you're about to learn why that's not always a good thing.

**Key features to measure:**
- Eye spacing and size relative to face width
- Nose width and length relative to face dimensions

- Face length-to-width ratio
- Cheekbone prominence
- Jaw width and definition

If your $V$ score is below $0.6$, congratulations, you're mathematically "distinctive." Whether that's good or bad depends on your tolerance for harsh mathematical truths.

## Component 4: Proportion Harmony (P) - The "Does Your Face Make Mathematical Sense" Test

Forget the golden ratio (science says it's bogus). Instead, we're looking at basic facial proportions that actually correlate with attractiveness ratings.

### Proportions That Actually Matter:
1. **Eye spacing**: Distance between eyes should be approximately one eye width
2. **Face length ratios**: Total face height roughly $1.3$-$1.4$ times face width
3. **Mouth positioning**: Mouth width roughly $1.2$-$1.5$ times nose width
4. **Vertical thirds**: Face divided into roughly equal thirds (hairline to eyebrows, eyebrows to nose, nose to chin)

### The Math:
$$\text{For each proportion: } \text{Deviation} = \frac{|\text{Your Ratio} - \text{Ideal Range Center}|}{\text{Ideal Range Width}}$$

$$P_{\text{component}} = \max(0, 1 - \text{Deviation})$$

$$P = \text{Average}(\text{All } P_{\text{components}})$$

**Translation:** If your facial proportions fall within normal human ranges, you score well. If your features are way out of proportion, the math will notice and judge accordingly.

### The Symmetry Reality Check:
$$\text{For key facial feature pairs: } \text{Asymmetry} = \frac{|\text{left measurement} - \text{right measurement}|}{\max(\text{left measurement}, \text{right measurement})}$$

$$S = 1 - \text{Average}(\text{All Asymmetries})$$

If your $S$ score is low, don't panic. Recent science shows it barely affects your attractiveness. Focus on being average and feminine instead.
